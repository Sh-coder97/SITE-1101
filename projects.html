Project 1 · SITE 1101

As Team 56, we planned and developed a set of digital logic gates during our coursework at ADA University. This project aimed to bridge theoretical knowledge with hands-on practice by transforming abstract concepts into real electronic implementations. We successfully constructed AND, OR, NOT, NAND, and XOR gates using fundamental electronic components, gaining practical insight into how hardware-level logic operates.

Project 2 · SITE 1101

In Team 56, we organized an interactive educational visit to a school, where we conducted a hands-on learning session for students. Throughout the workshop, we introduced and demonstrated algorithms such as Random, Square, and Back-and-Forth using simple, real-life examples. The session was designed to encourage logical reasoning and to help students build early computational thinking skills in an engaging and accessible way.
